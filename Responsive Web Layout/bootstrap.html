About This webpage

Cities in common generally refer to urban areas that share certain characteristics, such as economic, cultural, or historical traits, which may link them together in a broader regional or global context. These commonalities can be based on shared challenges, like urbanization, or on similarities in infrastructure, architecture, population density, or governance. In a broader sense, "cities in common" can also refer to networks or partnerships between cities, often in the form of international collaborations or sister city agreements. These relationships are meant to promote cultural exchange, economic development, and mutual learning.
